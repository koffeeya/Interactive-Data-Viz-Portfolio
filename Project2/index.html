Why Are Tribal Schools Struggling to Meet Student Needs?
Tribal schools are an important means to preserve and pass on the distinct cultures of Native American people.
But young people in tribal schools, particularly vulnerable populations, face immense challenges in keeping pace with their non-Native peers in academic outcomes. Their schools struggle to find the basic resources, staff, and funding to adequately meet their unique needs.

TRIBAL SCHOOL OUTCOMES
Students in tribal schools are falling behind, particularly vulnerable populations.

According to 2015-2016 tribal school report cards (the most recent data available from the BIE), only 12 schools out of 137 had a majority (50% or more) of students test "proficient" in math, and 26 schools proficient in reading.

By far, the widest gap is seen in the population of students with disabilities. Only 1 school had the majority of students with disabilities test proficient in reading, and 2 in math.
